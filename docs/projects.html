Summer 2020

Summer 2020 - the summer of quarantine. Though there were restrictions that prevented me from having the summer of my dreams, I made lemonade out of the lemons I was handed. I took Summer 2020 to build as a content creator: I worked with various organizations and grew my passion projects. It definitely was not my ideal summer, but it was a productive one nonetheless.

Addyk

Addyk is an apparel company created by USC alumna Adrienne Kim. Though the company first started producing tie-dye clothing (which became a trend during Summer 2020 due to WFH loungewear), it eventually expanded into other forms of clothing. The branding of the company was unique - grocery store meets clothing - which was why I was drawn to the company. Thus, during my free time, I helped create graphics for the social media (@addyk.s on Instagram).

Sparrows

I was able to be a Marketing Intern for a venture-backed social media start-up called Sparrows during this summer. I spearheaded the team in charge of setting up a blog for the website (stories.sparrow.co). As the team lead, I helped a lot where I was needed on the Content Production team, which often meant making graphics to go alongside the blog posts or graphics for social media to bring more traffic onto the blog.

Opportunime

Opportunime is a non-profit organization that provides resources for underprivileged college students. The CEO of the organization needed help providing information and resources on Instagram in an aesthetically pleasing manner, so I created graphics to post on the Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter five times every week for the summer.

Alpha Kappa Psi - Alpha Zeta

For the Fall 2020 semester, I was the Vice President of Public Relations for Alpha Kappa Psi. As a part of my role, I helped improve upon the USC Alpha Kappa Psi website and created content to post on the Instagram (@azakpsi) for the summer. These graphics would often demonstrate how brilliant the brothers of Alpha Zeta were.

Fall 2019

Fall 2019 was when I first began improving upon my design skills. Though I had experience here and there using Canva and had Photoshop downloaded onto my laptop, I really begun content creation because of Alpha Kappa Psi. However, the work I did for Alpha Kappa Psi mainly consisted of creating advertisement, flyers, and social media promotion posts. I started using the skills though for my class projects.

Marketing 450: Consumer Behavior

For my first marketing course at USC, I was given a project in which I would have to apply marketing concepts to solve any sort of issue. This was very reminiscent of the design thinking process. As a proposed solution to the problem of "all-nighters," a behavior that many college students engage in, we created a new mobile application that would mix elements of Blackboard & PocketPoints.
